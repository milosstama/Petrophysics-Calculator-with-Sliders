Archie's Equation
Calculates water saturation (Sw) from resistivity logs in clean (clay-free) reservoir rocks. Drag sliders or type exact values.
Formula Sw = [ (a × Rw) / (φm × Rt) ]1/n   |   F = a / φm   |   Sh = 1 − Sw

Key Assumptions & Limitations
Clean sand onlyClay minerals conduct electricity independently — Archie's will overestimate Sw in shaly sands. Use Waxman-Smits or Dual-Water model instead.
Rw calibrationMust be representative of the formation water at reservoir temperature. Get from produced water samples or SP log analysis.
m and nIdeally measured on core plugs in the lab. Defaulting to 2.0 introduces uncertainty — especially in carbonates.
InvasionRt must be the uninvaded resistivity. If your LLS ≈ LLD, invasion is minimal and Rt ≈ LLD.

Crossplot Rules of Thumb
Gas effectφD reads too high (gas less dense), φN reads too low (gas traps fewer neutrons). Crossover (φN < φD) is a gas indicator.
Shale effectBoth φD and φN read too high in shale. Similar separation, no crossover.
LithologyOn a neutron-density crossplot, points plot above the sandstone line for limestone/dolomite due to higher matrix density.

Which Vsh Formula to Use
Linear (conservative)Overestimates Vsh in older, well-consolidated rocks. Use as an upper bound or when in doubt.
Larionov TertiaryBest for young, uncompacted formations. Gives lower Vsh than linear — more optimistic.
Clavier0.33×(2^(2×IGR)−1). A middle-ground correction, widely used in the Western Canada Sedimentary Basin.
Always use min()The smallest Vsh from corrected methods is usually the most geologically defensible for pay estimation.
Net Pay Cutoffs
Apply porosity, water saturation, and shale volume cutoffs to determine whether a zone qualifies as net pay.
Net Pay Condition Zone is NET PAY if: φ ≥ φ_cutoff  AND  Sw ≤ Sw_cutoff  AND  Vsh ≤ Vsh_cutoff

Important Context
Cutoffs are field-specificAlways calibrated to production data and core analysis. Generic cutoffs are a starting point only.
Net-to-gross (NTG)NTG = net pay thickness / gross interval. A key input for STOIIP/GIIP volumetric calculations.
PermeabilityThese log cutoffs are proxies. Core plug k measurements are the ground truth for flow capacity.

Physical Meaning
What F tells youF = Ro/Rw — how many times more resistive is the rock (100% brine-saturated) versus the brine alone. Reflects pore connectivity.
F and permeabilityLow F (high φ, low m) generally correlates with better permeability, though F itself is not a permeability predictor.
Lab measurementMeasure Rw on a brine sample, flood core to 100% Sw, measure Ro. F = Ro/Rw. Plot log(F) vs log(φ) to get m and a from the slope and intercept.
